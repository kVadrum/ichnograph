## Picked (and why)
Zig `build.zig.zon`. The format is flat and stable enough for a small
regex pass: top-level `.name` (enum literal in Zig 0.14+, quoted string
in earlier releases) and `.version` (always quoted). The notable
gotcha ‚Äî `.minimum_zig_version` sharing a suffix with `.version` ‚Äî has
no substring collision because the dotted form is
`.minimum_zig_version`, not `.version`. Matches the established
shape of the recent language additions and keeps the rig's
language-coverage thread moving.

## Diff summary
Adds Zig to the detected-language matrix. Detector reads `name` and
`version` from `build.zig.zon`, accepting both the Zig 0.14+ enum-literal
form (`.name = .my_pkg`) and the older quoted-string form
(`.name = "my_pkg"`). Version is always a quoted string. The
`.minimum_zig_version` field ‚Äî a common adjacent key ‚Äî cannot
masquerade as `.version` because the leading-dot match anchors at
`.version` literally, and `.minimum_zig_version` doesn't contain that
sequence. A reviewer should sanity-check that the regex doesn't pick
up a dependency's own `.name` if a dep block were ever to include one;
in practice ZON dep entries use `.url`/`.hash`/`.path`/`.lazy`, not
`.name`, so the risk is theoretical.
