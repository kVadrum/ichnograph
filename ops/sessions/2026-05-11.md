## Considered
- GFM footnote reference stripping (`[^1]`, `[^api-note]`) in
  notes/readme summaries — fits the long line of inline-markdown
  strip fixes shipped over the past two weeks.
- Reference-link-definition skipping at the top of a README
  (`[label]: url` block before the description) — slightly broader
  scope, would touch the paragraph-extraction loop in readme.ts, not
  just the strip helper. Deferred.
- HTML entity decoding (`&amp;`, `&copy;`, `&mdash;`) — useful but
  pulls in a named-entity table to do correctly; either too narrow
  (a handful of names) or too broad (full table) for one cycle.
- Workspace awareness in the stack detector (CLAUDE.md open question)
  — explicitly too broad for a 15-min cycle.

## Picked (and why)
Footnote references are a clean, narrow extension of the existing
inline-markdown strip pipeline. The regex mirrors the shape of the
neighbouring reference-link strip, the failure-mode landed by the
negative lookahead is well-scoped (don't eat a `[^1]: …` definition
line), and the two new tests pin both the inclusion and the carve-out.
Same change applied symmetrically in notes.ts and readme.ts because
both share the strip pipeline by convention.

## Diff summary
Adds `/\[\^[^\]\s]+\](?!:)/g` to both inline-markdown strip helpers.
The negative lookahead for `:` distinguishes inline references
(`tool[^1].`) from definition lines (`[^1]: body`); only the former
collapse. Label characters are restricted to non-whitespace and
non-`]` to match what GFM accepts. No effect on output for files
without footnote syntax, so existing summaries are unchanged.
